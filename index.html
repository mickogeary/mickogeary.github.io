Making Coffee using a Moka Pot

What is a Moka pot?

A Moka pot is used to brew coffee by passing boiling water pressurized by steam up through ground coffee.

A standard Moka pot is normally:

  • relatively small in size (e.g. 200 ml volume)
  • aluminium (or stainless steel, or other alloys)
  • used over a flame, or on a stovetop

It consists of 3 main parts:

Boiler Chamber Metal Filter Collecting Chamber
  • the part at the base
  • has a small pressure release with a hexagonal nut valve at the side
  • has external (male) screw threads for joining to Collecting Chamber
  • the part at the centre
  • funnel shaped
  • the part at the top
  • has a filter, spout, lid, and a Bakelite handle
  • has internal (female) screw threads for joining to Boiler Chamber

You will need:

  • Moka Pot
  • finely ground coffee powder
  • heat source, e.g. stove
  • water (hot or cold, depending on preference)
  • something for handling hot Moka pot, e.g. a dish towel

Step 1

  • Add water to the Boiler Chamber

    • Note: the water level should not exceed the line just beneath the pressure valve (while Boiler Chamber is placed on level surface)

Step 2

  • Add finely ground coffee to the Metal Filter
  • Place filter so that it fits snugly into base, with the nozzle of filter funnel facing down and coffee facing up

    • Note: it is not necessary to heap, tamp, or pack the coffee

Step 3

  • Connect the Collecting Chamber to the Boiler Chamber which now has the filter (containing coffee powder) inserted
  • Place Moka pot on heat source, e.g. stove.
  • After a few minutes, e.g. upon hearing the "gurgling" noise, check that the liquid coffee is in the Collecting Chamber
  • Remove from heat

    • Note: it is preferable to place Moka pot so that handle is not directly over heat source

Pour coffee and enjoy !!!

(Don't forget to clean the constituent parts of Moka pot)
